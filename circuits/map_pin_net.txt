Example:

XTOP in out vdd vss top

.subckt top IN OUT VDD VSS
X1 IN n1 inv
X2 n1 OUT inv
.ends top

.subckt inv A Y
XBUF A Y buf
.ends inv

.subckt buf I O
.ends buf

Then:

python3 script.py --topinst XTOP test.sp inv:A

prints:

inv:A
-----
XTOP.X1                                           inv:A -> in
XTOP.X2                                           inv:A -> XTOP.n1

And:

python3 script.py --topinst XTOP test.sp XTOP.n1

prints:

XTOP.n1
-------
XTOP.X1                                           inv:Y -> XTOP.n1
XTOP.X2                                           inv:A -> XTOP.n1

Current deliberate limitations:

Hierarchy separator is period only.
No special global-net handling.
No primitive device reporting.
.subckt/.ends are case-insensitive.
Cell names, instance names, pin names, and net names are case-sensitive.
Subckt instance cell name is detected by known .subckt name and pin count.

One caveat: if a .subckt has zero body instances, querying its pins still works when that cell is instantiated, but querying internal connectivity obviously has nothing below it to report.
